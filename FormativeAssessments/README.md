# Formative Assessments Collection

This directory contains comprehensive formative assessments for various RTO courses. Each assessment follows a similar structure to ensure consistency and compliance with RTO standards.

## Assessment Structure

Each formative assessment includes:
- **Assessment Conditions**: Regulatory requirements and resource needs
- **Knowledge Assessment**: 50-55 theory questions covering legislation, safety, procedures, and operations
- **Calculations/Scenario Questions**: 2-5 practical calculation or scenario-based questions specific to the discipline

## Assessment Format

All assessments follow this consistent format:

### Knowledge Questions (Q1-55)
- Legislation and compliance
- Hazard identification and risk management
- Pre-operational checks
- Safe operating procedures
- Emergency procedures
- Post-operational requirements
- Equipment maintenance and defect reporting

### Calculations/Scenarios (2-5 questions)
Discipline-specific practical problems including:
- **Cranes/Rigging**: Load chart interpolation, sling angle de-rating, radius calculations
- **Forklifts/Telehandlers**: Load capacity, load center de-rating, attachment effects
- **Trucks**: Payload limits, GVM calculations, cycle times, volume calculations
- **Earthmoving**: Production rates, fuel consumption, excavation volumes
- **Safety**: Fall distances, clearance calculations, scenario responses
- **First Aid**: CPR compression counts, emergency response protocols

## Usage Guidelines

### For Trainers/Assessors
1. These are **formative assessments** designed for learning and practice
2. Use during training to identify knowledge gaps
3. Provide feedback and additional training where needed
4. Do not use as final/summative assessments without proper validation

### For Learners
1. Complete these assessments as part of your training
2. Review incorrect answers with your trainer
3. Use as study guides for final assessments
4. Practice calculations multiple times

### For RTO Administrators
1. Review and customize to match your specific training delivery
2. Ensure alignment with current legislation and standards
3. Update questions as regulations change
4. Validate against current Australian Standards and Codes of Practice

## Compliance Notes

### Important Disclaimers

**High Risk Work Licences**:
- Successful completion of these assessments alone does NOT result in a HRW Licence
- A Statement of Attainment is required to sit for the national licence assessment
- Practical demonstrations must be assessed in addition to knowledge assessments
- All HRW licences require renewal every 5 years

**Simulator Restrictions**:
- Simulators must NOT be used for assessment of HRW licence units
- Assessment must occur in workplace or simulated workplace conditions with real equipment

**Regulatory Requirements**:
- All assessments must satisfy requirements in the Standards for RTOs
- Assessment must be appropriate to language, literacy, and numeracy requirements
- Resources must include access to actual equipment, tools, and PPE used in industry
