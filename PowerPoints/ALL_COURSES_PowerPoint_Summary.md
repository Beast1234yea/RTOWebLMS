## TLILIC0009 - Portal Boom Crane (60 slides)

### SECTION 1: Introduction (7 slides)
1. **Title Slide** - TLILIC0009 Portal Boom Crane
2. **Course Overview** - HRW licence, portal boom crane operations, load charts
3. **Licensing Requirements** - Application process, 60 days, 5-year renewal
4. **What is a Portal Boom Crane** - Runway-mounted, slewing boom, container handling
5. **Types of Portal Cranes** - Ship-to-shore, rail-mounted, rubber-tyred
6. **Why Licensing** - High risk, serious incident potential, legal requirement
7. **Learning Outcomes** - Operate safely, interpret load charts, coordinate with riggers

### SECTION 2: Legislation (8 slides)
8. **WHS Legislation** - Acts, regulations, codes of practice
9. **PCBU Duties** - Safe equipment, trained operators, maintenance
10. **Worker Duties** - Hold licence, follow procedures, report defects
11. **Penalties** - Fines, prosecution, licence loss
12. **Hazard vs Risk** - Definitions and examples
13. **Risk Assessment** - Identification, evaluation, control
14. **Hierarchy of Control** - Elimination through to PPE
15. **Communication Requirements** - Rigger coordination, hand signals, radio

### SECTION 3: Crane Components (10 slides)
16. **Major Components** - Portal frame, runway, boom, slewing mechanism
17. **Boom Assembly** - Sections, sheaves, hoist ropes
18. **Slewing Mechanism** - 360-degree rotation, slew ring, motors
19. **Hoisting System** - Wire rope, drum, load block
20. **Trolley/Travel System** - Runway wheels, drive motors, rail guidance
21. **Controls** - Cabin controls, emergency stops, limit switches
22. **Safety Devices** - Load limiters, boom angle indicators, wind speed monitors
23. **Load Moment Indicator** - Prevents overload, alarm systems
24. **Emergency Lowering** - Manual/hydraulic backup systems
25. **Data Plate & Load Chart** - Capacity ratings, configuration limits

### SECTION 4: Load Charts & Calculations (9 slides)
26. **Understanding Load Charts** - Radius, capacity, boom angle
27. **Reading Charts** - Interpolation between values
28. **Radius Calculation** - Horizontal distance from slew center
29. **Load Weight** - Including rigging equipment weight
30. **De-Rating** - Wind, out-of-service conditions
31. **Rigging Weight** - Must be included in calculations
32. **Example Calculation 1** - Standard lift scenario
33. **Example Calculation 2** - Complex multi-component load
34. **Safety Margins** - Never exceed chart values

### SECTION 5: Pre-Operational Checks (8 slides)
35. **Pre-Start Importance** - Prevent failures, legal requirement
36. **Visual Inspection** - Structure, ropes, sheaves, runway
37. **Wire Rope Inspection** - Broken wires, wear, corrosion, kinks
38. **Functional Tests** - All motions, brakes, emergency stops
39. **Safety Device Tests** - Load limiter, limit switches, alarms
40. **Documentation** - Checklist completion, defect reporting
41. **Defect Response** - Tag out, report, don't operate
42. **Maintenance Records** - Statutory inspections, service history

### SECTION 6: Safe Operations (12 slides)
43. **Pre-Lift Planning** - Lift plan, hazard assessment, exclusion zones
44. **Communication** - Pre-job briefing, hand signals, radio procedures
45. **Load Preparation** - Rigging inspection, balance check, trial lift
46. **Lifting Sequence** - Take slack, trial lift, check stability, proceed
47. **Slewing with Load** - Smooth movements, watch swing, clear of obstacles
48. **Traveling on Runway** - Speed limits, track conditions, communication
49. **Load Placement** - Controlled lowering, rigger direction, final positioning
50. **Tandem Lifts** - Coordination, load sharing, communication critical
51. **Weather Conditions** - Wind limits, rain, visibility
52. **Emergency Stops** - When to use, aftermath procedures
53. **End of Shift** - Parking position, boom secured, crane secured
54. **Handover Procedures** - Log book, defects noted, next operator briefed

### SECTION 7: Emergencies (6 slides)
55. **Overload Situations** - Stop immediately, assess, redistribute
56. **Power Failure** - Emergency lowering, securing load
57. **Powerline Contact** - Stay in cabin, emergency services, ground clearance
58. **Fire Emergency** - Evacuation, emergency services
59. **Collision/Impact** - Stop operations, inspection before restart
60. **Summary & Assessment** - Key points, questions, practical preparation

## TLILIC0020 - Crane (Open) (70 slides)

### SECTION 1: Introduction (8 slides)
1. **Title** - Slewing Mobile Crane (Open Licence)
2. **Course Overview** - Unlimited capacity mobile cranes
3. **Licence Class** - C2 (Open), no weight restrictions
4. **Mobile Crane Types** - All terrain, rough terrain, truck-mounted
5. **Industry Applications** - Construction, infrastructure, heavy industry
6. **Why Advanced Training** - Large capacities, complex operations, high risk
7. **Course Structure** - Theory, calculations, extensive practical
8. **Assessment Requirements** - All components must pass

### SECTION 2: Legislation (10 slides)
9-18. Similar to other crane courses but emphasizing:
- Higher duty of care for large capacity equipment
- Lift plan legal requirements for complex lifts
- Notification requirements for certain lifts
- AS 2550 compliance for mobile cranes

### SECTION 3: Crane Components (12 slides)
19-30. Covering:
- Carrier and superstructure
- Boom configurations (main, fly jib, luffing jib)
- Outrigger systems (H-pattern, X-pattern, asymmetric)
- Counterweight configurations
- Load moment indicator/rated capacity limiter
- Anti-two block systems
- Boom angle indicators
- Wind speed monitors

### SECTION 4: Load Charts & Advanced Calculations (15 slides)
31-45. Including:
- Multiple chart selection (configuration-specific)
- Over-front vs over-side capacities
- Boom length and angle effects
- Jib de-rating calculations
- Working radius determination
- Complex rigging weight calculations
- Interpolation techniques
- Multi-crane lift calculations
- Dynamic loading factors

### SECTION 5: Ground Preparation & Setup (10 slides)
46-55. Covering:
- Ground bearing capacity assessment
- Crane mat selection and sizing
- Outrigger pressure calculations
- Setup on slopes (usually prohibited)
- Underground services identification
- Overhead clearances
- Travel route planning
- Setup documentation

### SECTION 6: Operations (10 slides)
56-65. Including:
- Lift planning process
- Pre-lift meetings
- Rigging coordination
- Boom extension sequences
- Load handling techniques
- Emergency procedures
- Multiple crane lifts
- Night operations
- Dismantling procedures
- Transport configurations

### SECTION 7: Summary (5 slides)
66-70. Course recap, assessment preparation, questions
