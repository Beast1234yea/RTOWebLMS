# PowerPoint Presentations - RTO Courses
## Complete Training Slide Decks

This directory contains comprehensive PowerPoint presentation outlines for 14 RTO courses. Each file provides slide-by-slide content ready to copy into PowerPoint.

---

## How to Use These Outlines

### Creating Your PowerPoint:
1. Open the markdown (.md) file for your course
2. Copy the content for each slide
3. Create new slides in PowerPoint
4. Paste content into slides
5. Add images, diagrams, and your branding
6. Customize colors and fonts to match your organization

### Slide Structure:
Each slide includes:
- **Slide title**
- **Bullet points** for content
- **[Image]** placeholders - insert relevant images/photos/diagrams
- **Presenter notes** (where applicable)

### Recommended PowerPoint Settings:
- **Slide size:** 16:9 widescreen
- **Font:** Sans-serif (Arial, Calibri) for readability
- **Font size:** Minimum 24pt for body text, 36-44pt for titles
- **High contrast:** Dark text on light background (or vice versa)
- **Images:** High quality, relevant to content
- **Consistency:** Use same template throughout

## Standard Presentation Structure

All presentations follow this consistent structure:

### Section 1: Introduction (5-8 slides)
- Title slide
- Course overview & outcomes
- Licensing/certification requirements
- Why the training matters
- Course structure & assessment

### Section 2: Legislation & Responsibilities (6-10 slides)
- WHS Act & Regulations
- PCBU responsibilities
- Worker duties
- Penalties & consequences
- Hazard vs risk
- Hierarchy of control

### Section 3: Equipment/Components (8-15 slides)
- Major components
- Safety systems
- Controls & instruments
- Data plates/capacity charts
- Inspection points

### Section 4: Theory/Calculations (8-12 slides)
- Load theory (for lifting equipment)
- Capacity calculations
- De-rating factors
- Practical examples
- Safety margins

### Section 5: Pre-Operational Checks (8-12 slides)
- Importance of inspections
- Walk-around checks
- Functional tests
- Defect reporting
- Documentation

### Section 6: Safe Operating Procedures (12-20 slides)
- Starting procedures
- Safe work practices
- Step-by-step operations
- Environmental considerations
- Parking & shutdown

### Section 7: Hazards & Emergencies (6-10 slides)
- Common hazards
- Emergency procedures
- Incident response
- First actions
- Reporting

### Section 8: Summary & Assessment Prep (2-5 slides)
- Key takeaways
- Assessment overview
- Questions
- Additional resources

## Customization Tips

### Adding Your Branding:
- Insert company logo on title slide and footers
- Use company colors for theme
- Add company contact information
- Include company-specific policies

### Making Slides Visual:
- **Photos:** Real equipment, work sites, proper techniques
- **Diagrams:** Component labels, load calculations, hand signals
- **Videos:** Embed short clips of operations, incidents (appropriate)
- **Icons:** Use to represent concepts (safety, warning, checklist)

### Interactive Elements:
- **Poll questions:** Test understanding during presentation
- **Activities:** Group discussions, hazard identification games
- **Demonstrations:** Show equipment, PPE, first aid techniques
- **Practice:** Calculations worksheets, scenarios

### Presenter Notes:
- Add detailed notes under each slide in PowerPoint
- Include:
  - Time allocation per slide
  - Key points to emphasize
  - Questions to ask participants
  - Common mistakes to discuss
  - Real-world examples

## Delivery Recommendations

### Timing:
- **Theory sessions:** 15-20 seconds per slide minimum
- **Complex topics:** Allow 1-2 minutes per slide
- **Interactive activities:** Budget extra time
- **Breaks:** Every 45-60 minutes (10-15 min breaks)

### Teaching Techniques:
- **Tell, Show, Do:** Explain concept, demonstrate, let participants practice
- **Questioning:** Check understanding regularly
- **Real examples:** Use incidents, case studies (anonymized)
- **Hands-on:** Get participants interacting with equipment
- **Scenarios:** Role-play emergency situations

### Assessment Integration:
- **Formative:** Quizzes throughout presentation
- **Practice:** Calculations worksheets alongside theory
- **Observation:** Note participant engagement and understanding
- **Feedback:** Provide immediate corrections
